              How To Use

              1. Fill in your item's current affixes. Check GA for any Greater Affixes and Enchanted for the affix modified by the Enchantress (at most one).
              2. Fill in up to 4 target affixes — the affixes you want on the finished item. Check Needs Improvement for affixes that are already present in your current item but at an unsatisfactory roll value — this tells the optimizer to treat the affix as missing and plan to re-roll it.
              3. GA preservation is automatic: any GA on a current affix that aligns with a target affix is protected throughout optimization. The optimizer will never recommend an action that risks losing it.
              4. Check Legendary if your item cannot have affixes removed (Legendary items skip the remove step and go straight to cube-reroll). The optimizer tracks whether the Enchantress is still available from the Enchanted checkbox in the current panel — if no affix is marked Enchanted, the one-time enchant is still available.
              5. Set Gear Slot to your item type to restrict suggestions to slot-legal affixes and reduce compute time. Leave it at Any to allow all affixes.
              6. Set Class to your character class to narrow the Adept skill pool to that class's skills (plus class-agnostic generals). Leave it at Any to keep every class's skills in the pool — useful for browsing but not realistic for actual crafting.
              7. Click Optimize. The recommended cube action appears with success probability and expected steps to a terminal outcome (success or GA loss).
              8. Perform the action in-game, then record it with Apply Last Outcome: pick the modification type (Added / Removed / Changed / Enchanted), the prism used (pre-filled from the recommendation), the source affix that changed, and the new affix that appeared. Click Apply Outcome and Optimize to update the item state and immediately re-optimize. Applying a result automatically clears Needs Improvement from any target affix that was just obtained.
              9. Click Reset to clear all state and start over.

              Settings — Slot Legality Overrides
              If an affix you want to target isn't appearing in the autocomplete (because the built-in slot legality table doesn't list it for your gear slot), click Settings in the toolbar to add a custom override. Specify the Slot, an optional Class restriction, and the affix name. The override is saved permanently and takes effect immediately — no page refresh required. Overrides that are already covered by the built-in table are rejected automatically. To remove an override, open Settings and click the × next to it.

              Current Limitations
              • Affix pools are based on prism categories. Slot-accurate roll weights and per-affix frequencies are not modeled, so some suggestions may be theoretically possible but practically rare.
              • Affix values and thresholds are not modeled — the optimizer targets affix identity only, not roll quality (e.g. it cannot require at least 1500 Maximum Life).
              • Gold and crafting material costs are not included in the objective. The optimizer minimizes expected cube steps only.
              • Expected Cube Steps counts steps until a terminal outcome — either success (all targets met with GAs preserved) or failure (a protected GA is lost). A lower step count alongside a lower success probability is not a contradiction: most runs end quickly via failure.
              • Residual cases that exceed the abstract-state or iteration budget return an approximate best-so-far result with explicit diagnostics. Increase Thinking Time to expand the budget; 0 uses the largest configured budget.

              Settings

              The Expected Cube Steps number is the optimizer's best estimate of how many cube actions you'll need on average. For complex starting states it can be loose — sometimes overshooting by 2–3×.

              When enabled, the optimizer runs simulated playthroughs after computing its initial estimate, and keeps simulating until the result is within ±10% of itself (adaptive mode, up to 2 min). This is the most accurate estimate available.

              The recommended action does not change — only the displayed step count. While verification runs, Optimize stays disabled and the status shows "Verifying…"; click Stop to abort early. The displayed number already includes a one-step refinement that runs automatically regardless of this setting.

              Add overrides to make an affix legal on a slot that it wouldn't normally appear on. Overrides are additive (allow-only) and survive a main-state Reset.
